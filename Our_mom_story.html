Our Mom's Story

Mrs Swaranjit Kaur

"Against all odds, she inspires us with her unwavering spirit and resilience, reminding us every day that love and courage can conquer the darkest moments."

Our Mom's Life Before ALS -

Our Mom was completely active during her lifetime, managing office, home, and other responsibilities due to her untiring attitude. She was always working, fit and fine, with just a few age-related problems like pain in her knees and legs. Everything went perfectly normal until January 2008.

Initial Symptoms and Diagnosis -

In January 2008, she started experiencing some physical difficulties, one after another. We consulted many doctors, and eventually, the diagnosis came out to be this incurable disease: ALS, or motor neuron disease. It was the most devastating news we had ever received, bringing our lives to a standstill. We consulted the best doctors in India and also sought advice from experts worldwide through the internet. However, there was no known cause or cure for this disease to date. One doctor told us during the diagnosis, "Today is better than tomorrow for her," advising us to live for today.

Early Efforts to Find a Cure -

During the first two years after the diagnosis, we tried many options in hopes of finding a miracle cure for this incurable disease. We explored alternative therapies such as Homeopathy, Ayurvedic, Panchkarma oil massages, speech therapy, physiotherapy, medicines, and renowned Vaid medicines from Bijnor, among others. Despite trying all these treatments, the disease continued to progress, affecting her legs, arms, speech, swallowing abilities, and eventually her breathing. By November 2010, she reached the stage where she needed a ventilator.

Transition to Ventilator Support -

In December 2010, we brought Mom home on a ventilator, despite resistance from doctors, nurses, and even some family members who believed it wasn't feasible to keep a patient on a ventilator at home in India. Our mother wanted to leave the ICU and return home. We stayed with her in the ICU, alternating every 2-4 hours, for one and a half months. During this time, we witnessed much negligence by doctors and nurses in the multi-room ICU ward and realized the commercialization of healthcare services. We sought guidance from the internet and the best medical professionals, but found no references or information on managing a patient on a ventilator at home.

Life on Ventilator at Home -

Despite the challenges, we decided to bring Mom home on a ventilator with the hope that she could spend her remaining time in a familiar and comfortable environment. With God's blessings and Mom's courage, she has now completed over seven years on a ventilator, starting from December 12, 2010, which is also her birthday. It wasn't an easy journey, and we faced many challenges and situations that drained us mentally, emotionally, and physically.

Emotional and Physical Challenges -

Presently, her whole body is immovable except for the movement in her eyes. She can't speak or eat, receiving her nutrition via a PEG since October 2010. In June 2016, we performed the fourth change of the PEG BRT at home. Despite her immobility, her senses are intact, and she is not in a vegetative state. She watches TV, enjoys religious programs, movies, and comedy shows, and is a fan of Amitabh Bachchan, Salman Khan, and Kapil Sharma. It was an honor to receive a personalized letter from Mr. Amitabh Bachchan for our beloved mother in 2017.

Recognition and Support -

There are very few people and limited medical information available about ALS in India. Long-term care for ventilator patients is often neglected, even in the best hospitals. Infections acquired in multi-patient ICU units are more dangerous than those acquired at home. Bedsores, a common issue for bedridden patients in hospitals, can result from staff negligence. At home, Mom has never experienced bedsores despite being bedridden for over seven years.

Awareness and Advocacy Efforts -

We have moved from questioning "Why it happened to us" to focusing on helping other patients and their families manage this devastating disease. We started writing necessary information on our website and adding videos to our YouTube channel. In 2015, we created the 'ALS care & support' WhatsApp group and continue to update our site and YouTube channel whenever we find time. The purpose of creating this platform is to spread awareness about ALS and its management in India.


Satvinder & Sukhvinder ( Daughters)
