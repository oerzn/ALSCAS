1. Just diagnosed.

  • Consult at least 3-4 neurologists to confirm the diagnosis and rule out mimic diseases. In addition to recommended tests, consider checking CPK Total, Acetylcholine receptor, MUSK antibody/antigen levels, ANA profile, CACNA, PACNA levels for assessing autoimmune condition with probable IgIV treatment available. Also, get genetic testing done to understand any specific gene mutation impact in case of familial form of ALS.
  • An ALS support group
  • Maintain a positive environment at home, and plan some get togethers to keep spirits high.
  • Nutrition is extremely important, please focus on the diet. Include good protein supplements like Ensure, whey protein etc in your diet. Get tested for Vit D and Vit B12 levels and keep them in upper range of sufficient category. Adding ashwagandha and Vit C in daily routine may be helpful.
    • Engage in light exercise to remain active. Avoid strenuous physiotherapy or hard massage. Instead, gentle oil application is Ok .

2. Mobility issues

  • Prevent falls at all cost. Use assistance and aids like walkers, wheelchairs, and railings.
  • Customised utensils, splints and clothing are available to stay independent to the extent possible.
  • If bedridden, opting for an airbed early can improve blood circulation and relieve pain due to stiffness in the body.

3. Speech/Swallowing issues

  • Monitor closely if coughing happens while swallowing food, this could be an indication of food particles going into trachea. This can pose risk, if unattended.
  • Consider voice banking early when speech issues arise. This can help regenerate PALS’s own voice later using PALS’s own, using technology tools when speech deteriorates.
  • Consult therapists aware of MND/ALS- Practice speech and swallowing exercises and tips on a daily basis.
  • Use communication tools like bells, eye trackers(Tobii), laser pens, alphabet boards etc as needed.

4. Nutrition issues

  • Consider a feeding tube if any of the following are true: Need to churn/blend food, taking 30 minutes and more to eat, eating reduced portions, choking episodes, or significant weight loss. Please consult a gastroenterologist for further evaluation and plan for PEG tube feeding. If surgery is not an option, a Ryle tube(use silicon) is an alternative.

5. Breathing concerns

  • Regularly monitor SpO2 levels through pulse-oximeter. If Spo2 levels drop below 96 when awake or 94 or below when sleeping, get ready and prepare home with BiPAP and oxygen concentrator to avoid emergencies.
  • To manage phlegm, Cough assist machine, suction machine can be a valuable device.
  • Learn how to operate these devices, manage circuit attachments and required settings.

6. Non-invasive ventilation

  • Gradually increase the use of breathing assistive devices. You will need it at sleep stage first. Ensure your device has BiPAP ST mode with AVAPS/IVAPS features.
  • If phlegm or infections occur often, or BiPAP use is needed 24/7, consider planning for a tracheostomy to prevent need for emergency intubation. Importantly, learn the tracheal suction procedure. Refer to ALSCAS resources for more information.

7. Home ICU setup

  • Refer to the ALSCAS home ICU resources.
  • Visit a ALS family in your location with BiPAP/Trachea experience to familiarize yourself
  • Have backups for all essential devices and attachments. Learn yourself and train your nurses/attendants/caregivers on managing pneumonia and other critical issues.

8. Caregiver must take regular breaks

  • Caregiver play a vital role in the ALS journey, providing essential physical, medical, and emotional support, including assistance with mobility, personal care, and communication.
  • Caregivers must take small breaks frequently to manage stress and avoid burnout. They should have their own backup to take care of PALS for a few hours.

9. Multidisciplinary specialists

    Get in touch with competent neurologists, physicians, physiotherapists, speech therapist, gastroenterologist, pulmonologist and ENT consultant and consult them as needed .
  • Neurologist – Consult for ALS diagnosis, disease progression, and treatment options.
  • Physician – Manage general health and coordinate care with specialists.
  • Physiotherapist – Assist with mobility, muscle strength, and physical therapy.
  • Speech Therapist – Help with speech difficulties and communication strategies.
  • Gastroenterologist – Address swallowing issues and nutrition management.
  • Pulmonologist – Monitor and manage respiratory function and breathing support.
  • ENT Consultant – Evaluate swallowing, speech, and airway-related concerns.
  • Occupational Therapist – Provide adaptive strategies for daily activities and recommend assistive devices for better independence.

10. Financial support and employment

  • As the disease progresses, PALS would need would need a number of assistive devices at various stages. One should plan the funds well, and take timely decisions on financial matters.
  • Stay employed if you are, government policies may entitle you to full salary. Refer to ALSCAS website for guidance.

ALS affects the body, not the mind. One can live a long, fulfilling life with proper management of nutrition, and breathing by taking PEG & BiPAP/ventilator decisions at the right time. By using appropriate communication tools, one can also continue to live a well-connected social and professional life for years, even while being on PEG and Ventilator.

Our ALSCAS family is a living example of hundreds of patients thriving for months/years in a home ICU setup. You are not alone in the journey. Till the time there is no cure, there is care and support.
