Assistive Devices for ALS Patients

Assistive technology enhances quality of life by supporting independence, communication, mobility, and daily care.

Mobility Assistance (Limb Impairment)

Powered Wheelchair
Facilitates mobility and independence with customizable controls for ALS patients with limited strength.
Ankles Foot Braces (AFOs)
Foot drop happens in ALS, AFOs Help patient to walk, avialble in different sizes
Stick
Used in initial stage of walking difficulty
Recliner Wheelchair with head support
Wheelchair with reclining capabilty and head support give PALS great comfort, some have Commode too, which is aslo very useful.
Walker
Provides stability and support during walking for those with early-stage muscle weakness.
Walker
Helps in Toileting assistance, Transfer ease.

Communication Aids (Speech Impairment)

Tobii Eye Tracker
Allows ALS patient to control computers and communicate through eye movement, ideal for severe speech impairment. It have various models.
Communication Chart
Simple and effective tool for basic communication using letters, symbols, or phrases.

Feeding Support (Swallowing Issues)

Feeding Assist Tools
Adaptive utensils, plate guards, and feeding robots can help patients eat independently and safely.
Feeding Tube (PEG/NGT)
Provides essential nutrition and hydration when swallowing becomes unsafe or inefficient.

Respiratory Assistance

BiPAP Machine
Supports breathing by delivering pressurized air during inhalation and exhalation.
Suction Machine
Helps remove mucus and secretions, reducing the risk of aspiration and infection.

Daily Living and Comfort

Adjustable Hospital Bed
Improves comfort, posture, and ease of caregiving with motorized features.
Patient Lift
Safely transfers individuals between bed and chair, reducing caregiver strain.
